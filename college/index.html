Unit 1

The Online World

Unit 1 was the first of our exam units. In this unit we leant about the the internet, the services that are provided on the internet, how data is sent accross a network and to the internet and some of the threats that are present when using the internet. for example one specific topic that was covered was email methods such as SMTP, POP3 and IMAP as well as the store and forward method.

Unit 10

Database Development

Unit 10 focused on designing and creating an Access database to meet the needs of a customer. We were tasked with creating an employee database for an engineering company that had recently expanded their premisis to include a new building. The company wanted a relational data base to store the employee information and the office and department information. the database also included a main menu to make user navigation easier as well as a login page to prevent unauthorised access and close the database should the user try and close the login screen or enter the wrong password.

Unit 13

Website Development

Unit 13 was combined with Unit 3. The website we designed for this unit also served as our digital portifolio. We started by outlineing the navigation structure of two different website designs showing how each page connected together and what links and content there was on each page. I then created a wireframe to outline the basic look of my two website designs. We then gathered assets that we would include in our website such as images, videos and other files such as PDFs or ZIP files. These assets were recorded in a asset table where their names, descriptions, origins and storage locations were recorded. We then decided on what design we were going to use for our website and justifyed why we chose the design before moving on to creating our websites.

Unit 14

Installing and Maintaining Computer Hardware

Unit 14 focused on the process and implications of installing and upgrading computer hardware. For example how to appropriately prevent electrostatic discharge, the risks involved when installing or maintaining hardware and some of the problems that my arise as a result of upgrading hardware such as bottlenecking, data loss and incompatablility. We also covered network setup, use and maintainence by creating a small local network between three computers using a switch as well as checking unknown network cables to determine if they are either straight, crossover or faulty cables.
